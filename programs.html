Programs

We typically hold meetings on the last reasonable Monday of the month. To peruse and register for upcoming events, or peruse past events, please see our Meetup site. Our meeting tradition is to go around the room and give each participant a chance to answer "What is the best/worst thing that happened/will happen to me in my private/personal life during the last/next month?" Current Programs
Book Club

Every two months, the group chooses a data-oriented book to all read, via survey. These books are written for a wide audience, so anyone can participate. We women are busy, and we never require that the book be finished. At our best, we provide alternate material, such as a TED talk, by the same author, for a shorter time frame. Julia Kuznetsova moderates our Book Club.

Reverse Book Club

Every two months, data scientistas take turns sharing about a non-fiction read.

Annual Event

In October, the last month of the meeting year, the Society hosts an annual event. In 2017, the Future Fair featured a panel of six guests from surrounding colleges and training programs. You can see our video here. In 2018, we held a member survey and un-contest of analyzing survey results. To learn abou the 2019 Future Fair, see the Future Fair page.

Annual Planning and Other Planning Meetings

The meeting in November is dedicated to reviewing metrics from the previous year and choosing future goals and leaders. This meeting, and any other meeting to plan events, are open to anyone, always.

Partner Presentations

Women in this field are so rare that often they work on all-but-totally-male teams. To get women the experience and pleasure of working on a technical project with another woman, we encourage partners to work on a project and present together. In fact, having a partner is a requirement for any presenter.

Amplification Project

A member may share a capstone project (along with GitHub repository) to the group, and the group will explore how it can be amplified.

TED Talks

Similarly to Book Club, TED Talks prompt discussion among members. Everyone can watch an un-announced video (preferably of a women) and then respond freshly.
